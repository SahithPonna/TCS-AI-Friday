Title: Sample FATF AML/CFT Framework Snippet
Effective Date: 2019-06-01
Jurisdiction: Global (FATF Recommendations)
Hierarchy: AML/CFT > Customer Due Diligence > Ongoing Monitoring

Sample Provision (Illustrative Text)
Financial institutions shall conduct ongoing due diligence on the business relationship and scrutinise transactions undertaken throughout the course of that relationship to ensure that transactions are consistent with the institutionâ€™s knowledge of the customer, their business and risk profile, including, where necessary, the source of funds.

Obligation Summary:
1. Implement risk-based ongoing monitoring of customer transactions and relationships.
2. Use automated and manual tools to detect unusual or suspicious patterns.
3. Escalate and investigate alerts, documenting rationale and outcomes.
4. File suspicious transaction reports (STRs) with the competent authority where suspicion is formed.

Title: Sample EMIR Trade Reporting Snippet
Effective Date: 2014-02-12
Jurisdiction: European Union (ESMA / EMIR)
Hierarchy: EMIR > Trade Reporting > OTC Derivatives

Sample Provision (Illustrative Text)
Counterparties and CCPs shall ensure that the details of any derivative contract they have concluded, modified or terminated are reported to a registered trade repository no later than the working day following the conclusion, modification or termination of the contract.

Obligation Summary:
1. Report all in-scope derivative contracts, including lifecycle events, to an approved trade repository.
2. Ensure T+1 reporting timeliness and data completeness and accuracy.
3. Reconcile reported data against internal books and records.
4. Maintain governance and controls over trade reporting, including periodic testing.
